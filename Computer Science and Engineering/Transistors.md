there are three types of material based on electrical conductivity - conductors, resistors, semi-conductors. semi conductors conduct electricity in certain conditions. example doped silicon.

silicon is a semi-conductor. silicon has 4 valence electrons. we can modify silicon to work as a better conductor by doping it with either boron(3 valence electrons) of phosphorus(5 valence electrons). doping it with boron creates an excess of holes(lack of electrons/negative charges; rather abundance of positive charges) called a p-type semiconductor. doping it with phosphorus creates an excess of electron making it an n-type semiconductor.

doping can be roughly visualised as - adding atoms of other elements in a 3d silicon lattice.

magic begins when we place differently doped silicon lattices back to back. because of the opposing polarities, they form a junction where the electrons move over and fill the poles, making one half of the junction positively charged and the other negatively charged. this results in a small voltage across the junction. this entire piece of silicon acts like a switch, it allows current to pass only in one direction while blocking it from the other direction. 

on placing the junctions back to back, we get different types of transistors, i.e n-p-n or p-n-p transistors. the portions of these transistors are named collector(moderately doped), base(lightly doped middle portion containing the opposite flavour of the dope. i.e, p in n-p-n or n in np-n-p transistors), emitter(heavily doped).

these transistors can be used to form switches and logic gates by connecting them in various configurations, which form the fundamental units of the computer hardware
